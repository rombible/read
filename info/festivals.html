Festivals

The main festivals: Pesach (Passover), Shavuot (Weeks or Pentecost), and Sukkot (Tabernacles, Tents or Booths)

 

[late rain, dry season starts]

Passover (PESACH): 14th Aviv (Nisan)

- Jesus's death

- priest with two showbreads at the top of the temple to let people know it is time to remove east from houses

- white cloth hung to let the diasporas know there is a room available

- the barley started to get ripe

- 30 men slaughter their own lambs and give the blood to the priests and the blood was poured to the bottom of the alter. Each lamb was consumed (Seder = order) by about 10-20 people for 6 hours until midnight Afterwards, go up to the roof top and sing psalms

- Seder : unleavened bread, lamb roasted pearced in pomegranate wood, bitter herb, brown sauce, boiled egg, wine, salt

- tied up barley

Unleavened bread (HAG HAMATZOT): 14th Aviv (Nisan), 7 days

- cleaning for a week before this festival

- whitewash the graves

- purification water - with cedar wood, hyssop, and scarlet wool burned with red heifer in the water from Siloam pool (Numbers 19:1-10 )

 

FIRSTFRUITS (YOM HABIKKURIM): The day after the first Sabbath after Passover - Presenting a sheaf of the first barley harvest as a wave offering; making a burnt offering and a grain offering.

- start of the barley harvest - wave offering (Leviticus 23:10-11) - from Gennetsaret

- offer grain offering - a bread with oil and frankincense (Leviticus 2:14-15)

- resurrection

WEEKS (PENTECOST=50thday in Greek) (SHAVUOT or HAG HASHAVUOT) : Sivan, May/June, 49 days after Firstfruits, FEAST OF THE HARVEST

PENTECOST - 50 days after Firstfruits

- the law was given in OT, the Holy Spirit came down

- new era starts

- two bread and two lambs are offered

- wheat harvest (white for harvest)

[early rain, rainy season starts]

TRUMPETS (ROSH HASHANA): 1 Tishri,

- new year's day - torches are lit up to let people know

- trumphet of the horn of a male sheep

- the period of fear and awe from this day to the day of Atonement

- Two side priests blow silver trumpets with short sounds while the middle one blows the ram's horn long at the purity/justice gate

- 10 more animals are sacrificed: 1 young bull, 1 male ram, 7 sheep, 1 goat

- the top of the temple. - trumpet blowing at every Sabbath, the first day of month, the first day of year

 

DAY OF ATONEMENT (YOM KIPUR): 10 Tishri

- 24 hours fasting (m 13 years, f 12 years older)

- most holy day

- The high priest had to live within Jerusalem

- The day before the atonement day, the high priest reading and teaching the representatives Torah

- The High priest taking oath before Sanhedrin

- First, for the high priest first. Go up to the Parva chamber (north east of priest court) where the salt for sacrifice was stored. In that room, there is a cleansing bath. After taking the bath the high priest wore white clothing with a white belt.

- Put his hand on the bull and confess his sin and the priests, his family.

- Pick an azazel goat through a lot = one as a sacrifice for God and the other to the widerness for the sin of the people.

- The high priest put his hands on the goat for God and the Azazel goat. (a red cloth on the horn of the Azazel and, on the neck of the one for God.)

- After slaughtering the bull, take the blood, take the ash on south west side, With the ash on his right and the incense on his left hand, he enters into the holy of holy

- Put the incense on top of the ash near the arc of the Lord

- After a short prayer, comes out of the holy of holy. The people waiting outside will be joyful.

- The high priest will go back to the holy of holy with the blood of the bull (for him and his family) from the assistant priest. Up once, and seven times downward.

- Sprinkle the blood in the same way in front of the curtain between the holy and the holy of holy.

- Now after killing the goat for God, he bring its blood to the holy of the holy and sprinkle it in the same way. And then mix the blood of the cow and the goat and sprinkle it again at the curtain.

- And smear the blood to the four horns at the incense place and sprinkle it in the same way there. By this the high priest, the holy of the holy, and the incense place, the people become holy.

- The rest of the blood will be poured out on the south east side of the alter.

- The Azazel goat will be led from the priest court to the Nicarno gate, into the women's court, purity/just gate, into the Susan gate (golden, mercy gate), to Kidron valley, Olive mountain, toward East

- The goat will be pushed backward on the Montar mountai

- The ash of the bull and the goat was completely burned at the olive mountain to conclude the atonement.

- After atonement sacrifice, the women wear white and go to the vinyards and dance in a circle.

- azazel goat

TABERNACLES (SUKKOTH): 15-22 Tishri, the fruit harvest (grapes & olives)

- storing of the harvest

- Sleep in the tents, remembering God's grace through Exodus

- Sacrifices are related to the number 7 (complete). 14 Rams, 98 sheeps, 336 eba flour, 7th month. For 7 days 70 bulls were offerred.

- Every morning, people go to a town called Motza (west of Jerusalem) to got get willow branches and set them up next to the altar south west in the temple. The branch should lean toward the altar. At the altar, the drink offering (water and wine) was offerred by priests.

- The people circled around the alter anti-clockwise reading Ps 118:25 Please LORD, deliver! Please LORD, grant us success! = Hosanna (hoshiya na) = save, please.

- Only during the seven days, men, women, and even children can go to the priest court.

- In the temple, people hit the willow branches on the ground and get the leaves fall down.

- Drink offering - usually only wine is used. During this festival, wine with water is offered.

- At Siloam pool, the high priest with the priests get the water and go through the water gate on the south side. Levites with trumpets welcome the priests with water. The water on the willow and the wine on the alter overflo

- On the last day of the feast, at the women's court, overnight all night celebration of joy.

- Old rabbis dance, sing, juggle torches all night like children while women watch them. The four candles high up were lit by boy priests with olive oil.

- The two priests blow trumpets three times, 10 steps down and blow them again three times, 5 more steps down and again three blows. Arriving at the purity/justice gate, turn around toward the holy and announce "Lord, the gentiles face East and worship the Sun, but our eyes are towards you." With a song of praising God who forgives sins, the festival finishes. Old Rabbis will shout/sing "Oh this Joy! The young, devout, wise don't dishonor the old." Other people will respond "Oh this Joy, with the old, we can wash our sins." And everyone shouts "The ones who sinned when young who are forgiven are blessed."

- On the last day, make 7 circles around the altar, saying Hosanna, next to the dying willow. Jesus shouted out because it is very noisy - women, children, hosanna.

- Praying for the early rain after the long dry season. on the 8th day of the feat of tents (shemini atzeret) - a separate festival day. The prayer for the early rain starts.

 

* The sacrifices on every first day of the month: two bulls, 1 male ram, 7 sheep, 1 goat as sin offering for the whole month.
