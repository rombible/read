Temple

959 BC   Solomon's Prayer of Temple Dedication

722 BC   Israel Led into Captivity

586 BC   The Fall of Jerusalem

535 BC   Temple Work Begins

515 BC   Completion and Dedication of the Temple

165 BC   Hasmonean expansion

17  BC     Herod the Great expansion for 46 years

70  AD    Destruction of the temple by Roman Titus army

 

Gates

E: Shushan gate - named after the capital of Persia, Mercy gate, Golden gate, Azazel goat passed through once a year on Atonement day. The ash from daily burnt offering is thrown through this gate.

S: Double gate - Beautiful gate, For normal people. 30 steps (every third step is wider than others). Enter to the right and exit to the left gate except for those with sad occasions.

   Triple gate - for priests

   - between those two gates, there were a washing pool and a lower court for 23 elders lower in rank compared with Sanhedrin

N: North gate - for sheep

W: Robinson's arch - a path to the royal court

   Barclay's gate - the path for sacrifices at the temple

   Wilson's arch - for the rich and the royal

   Warren's gate - the path close to the holy of holies

  

Solomon platform, Hasmonian platform, Herod's platform - expansion except for East 

 

Outer court

Royal porch - 2 stories, 4 rows, 162 pillars, as a meeting place and for forums, people of Hellenism and pro-Rome gathered

Market - forum, selling sacrifice animals, business, money exchanges, money lending, meeting place, Annas (the high priest, Caiaphas' father-in-law) controlled the market - overcharging and using the money as a bribe to Rome

Solomon's porch - Judaism and religeous experts gathered, new rabbis including Jesus taught here

Top of the temple - Jesus was tempted, a horn was blown on new year's day, the first day of the month, and sabbath day

Sanhedrin - the high court of 71 (23 elders*3+1+high priest)

Two Huldah gates - named after the prophetess wife of Shallum

The court of gentile - between holdah gates and the gentile partition (warnings in Latin and Greek). a purification pool before entering the temple.

 

Inner Court

Women's court - the high-rank rabbis taught (Jesus a week before his death). 13 offering box of trumpet shape. 4 candle posts which were used on the last day of the festival of Tents

 - Wood chamber : wood for burnt offering, unclean priests stayed here

 - Lepers' chamber : purification for lepers

 - Olive oil and wine chamber

 - Nazirites chamber - shave head and burn the hairs here

  - Women's gate (S) for normal people, Purity/Justice gate (E) for the rich and ritually clean people, music gate (N) for the Levi choir, Nicanor gate to enter the temple (heavy copper door that needed 20 men to open)

The court of Israel - men met priests with their sacrifices

the court of Priests - two steps from Israel's court, alter, slaughter table, laver (water tank)

 

Outside the temple

Antonia fortress - where Pilot questioned Jesus, Romans watching over the people at the temple

Israel's pool - to wash sacrifice sheep

Bethzatha pool - with five covered walkways
