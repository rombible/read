1 The heavens and the earth1 were completed with everything that was in them.2 2 By3 the seventh day God finished the work that he had been doing,4 and he ceased5 on the seventh day all the work that he had been doing. 3 God blessed the seventh day and made it holy6 because on it he ceased all the work that he7 had been doing in creation.8

The Creation of Man and Woman

4 This is the account9 of the heavens and

the earth10 when they were created – when the Lord God11 made the earth and heavens.12

5 Now13 no shrub of the field had yet grown on the earth, and no plant of the field14 had yet sprouted, for the Lord God had not caused it to rain on the earth, and there was no man to cultivate the ground.15 6 Springs16 would well up17 from the earth and water18 the whole surface of the ground.19 7 The Lord God formed20 the man from the soil of the ground21 and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life,22 and the man became a living being.23

8 The Lord God planted an orchard24 in the east,25 in Eden;26 and there he placed the man he had formed.27 9 The Lord God made all kinds of trees grow from the soil,28 every tree that was pleasing to look at29 and good for food. (Now30 the tree of life31 and the tree of the knowledge of good and evil32 were in the middle of the orchard.)

10 Now33 a river flows34 from Eden35 to

water the orchard, and from there it divides36 into four headstreams.37 11 The name of the first is Pishon; it runs through38 the entire land of Havilah, where there is gold. 12 (The gold of that land is pure;39 pearls40 and lapis lazuli41 are also there). 13 The name of the second river is Gihon; it runs through42 the entire land of Cush.43 14 The name of the third river is Tigris; it runs along the east side of Assyria.44 The fourth river is the Euphrates.

15 The Lord God took the man and placed45 him in the orchard in46 Eden to care for it and to maintain it.47 16 Then the Lord God commanded48 the man, “You may freely eat49 fruit50 from every tree of the orchard, 17 but51 you must not eat52 from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, for when53 you eat from it you will surely die.”54

18 The Lord God said, “It is not good for the man to be alone.55 I will make a companion56 for him who corresponds to him.”57 19 The Lord God formed58 out of the ground every living animal of the field and every bird of the air. He brought them to the man to see what he would59 name them, and whatever the man called each living creature, that was its name. 20 So the man named all the animals, the birds of the air, and the living creatures of the field, but for Adam60 no companion who corresponded to him was found.61 21 So the Lord God caused the man to fall into a deep sleep,62 and while he was asleep,63 he took part of the man’s side64 and closed up the place with flesh.65 22 Then the Lord God made66 a woman from the part he had taken out of the man, and he brought her to the man. 23 Then the man said,

“This one at last67 is bone of my bones

and flesh of my flesh;

this one will be called68 ‘woman,’

for she was taken out of69 man.”70

24 That is why71 a man leaves72 his father and mother and unites with73 his wife, and they become a new family.74 25 The man and his wife were both naked,75 but they were not ashamed.76
