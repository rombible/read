III. Job’s Search for Wisdom (28:1-28)

No Known Road to Wisdom1

1 “Surely2 there is a mine3 for silver,

and a place where gold is refined.4

2 Iron is taken from the ground,5

and rock is poured out6 as copper.

3 Man puts an end to the darkness;7

he searches the farthest recesses

for the ore in the deepest darkness.8

4 Far from where people live9 he sinks a shaft,

in places travelers have long forgotten,10

far from other people he dangles and sways.11

5 The earth, from which food comes,

is overturned below as though by fire;12

6 a place whose stones are sapphires13

and which contains dust of gold;14

7 a hidden path15 no bird of prey knows –

no falcon’s16 eye has spotted it.

8 Proud beasts17 have not set foot on it,

and no lion has passed along it.

9 On the flinty rock man has set to work18 with his hand;

he has overturned mountains at their bases.19

10 He has cut out channels20 through the rocks;

his eyes have spotted21 every precious thing.

11 He has searched22 the sources23 of the rivers

and what was hidden he has brought into the light.

No Price Can Buy Wisdom

12 “But wisdom – where can it be found?

Where is the place of understanding?

13 Mankind does not know its place;24

it cannot be found in the land of the living.

14 The deep25 says, ‘It is not with26 me.’

And the sea says, ‘It is not with me.’

15 Fine gold cannot be given in exchange for it,

nor can its price be weighed out in silver.

16 It cannot be measured out for purchase27 with the gold of Ophir,

with precious onyx28 or sapphires.

17 Neither gold nor crystal29 can be compared with it,

nor can a vase30 of gold match its worth.

18 Of coral and jasper no mention will be made;

the price31 of wisdom is more than pearls.32

19 The topaz of Cush33 cannot be compared with it;

it cannot be purchased with pure gold.

God Alone Has Wisdom

20 “But wisdom – where does it come from?34

Where is the place of understanding?

21 For35 it has been hidden

from the eyes of every living creature,

and from the birds of the sky it has been concealed.

22 Destruction36 and Death say,

‘With our ears we have heard a rumor about where it can be found.’37

23 God understands the way to it,

and he alone knows its place.

24 For he looks to the ends of the earth

and observes everything under the heavens.

25 When he made38 the force of the wind

and measured39 the waters with a gauge.

26 When he imposed a limit40 for the rain,

and a path for the thunderstorm,41

27 then he looked at wisdom42 and assessed its value;43

he established44 it and examined it closely.45

28 And he said to mankind,

‘The fear of the Lord46 – that is wisdom,

and to turn away from evil is understanding.’”47
