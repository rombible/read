A Protest of Innocence

1 And Job took up his discourse again:1

2 “As surely as God lives,2 who has denied me justice,3

the Almighty, who has made my life bitter4 –

3 for while5 my spirit6 is still in me,

and the breath from God is in my nostrils,

4 my7 lips will not speak wickedness,

and my tongue will whisper8 no deceit.

5 I will never9 declare that you three10 are in the right;

until I die, I will not set aside my integrity!

6 I will maintain my righteousness

and never let it go;

my conscience11 will not reproach me

for as long as I live.12

The Condition of the Wicked

7 “May my enemy be like the wicked,13

my adversary14 like the unrighteous.15

8 For what hope does the godless have when he is cut off,16

when God takes away his life?17

9 Does God listen to his cry

when distress overtakes him?

10 Will he find delight18 in the Almighty?

Will he call out to God at all times?

11 I will teach you19 about the power20 of God;

What is on the Almighty’s mind21 I will not conceal.

12 If you yourselves have all seen this,

Why in the world22 do you continue this meaningless talk?23

13 This is the portion of the wicked man

allotted by God,24

the inheritance that evildoers receive

from the Almighty.

14 If his children increase – it is for the sword!25

His offspring never have enough to eat.26

15 Those who survive him are buried by the plague,27

and their28 widows do not mourn for them.

16 If he piles up silver like dust

and stores up clothing like mounds of clay,

17 what he stores up29 a righteous man will wear,

and an innocent man will inherit his silver.

18 The house he builds is as fragile as a moth’s cocoon,30

like a hut31 that a watchman has made.

19 He goes to bed wealthy, but will do so no more.32

When he opens his eyes, it is all gone.33

20 Terrors overwhelm him like a flood;34

at night a whirlwind carries him off.

21 The east wind carries him away, and he is gone;

it sweeps him out of his place.

22 It hurls itself against him without pity35

as he flees headlong from its power.

23 It claps36 its hands at him in derision

and hisses him away from his place.37
