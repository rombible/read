1 The Lord will certainly have compassion on Jacob;1 he will again choose Israel as his special people2 and restore3 them to their land. Resident foreigners will join them and unite with the family4 of Jacob. 2 Nations will take them and bring them back to their own place. Then the family of Jacob will make foreigners their servants as they settle in the Lord’s land.5 They will make their captors captives and rule over the ones who oppressed them. 3 When the Lord gives you relief from your suffering and anxiety,6 and from the hard labor which you were made to perform, 4 you will taunt the king of Babylon with these words:7

“Look how the oppressor has met his end!

5 The Lord has broken the club of the wicked,

the scepter of rulers.

6 It9 furiously struck down nations

with unceasing blows.10

It angrily ruled over nations,

oppressing them without restraint.11

7 The whole earth rests and is quiet;

they break into song.

8 The evergreens also rejoice over your demise,12

as do the cedars of Lebanon, singing,13

‘Since you fell asleep,14

no woodsman comes up to chop us down!’15

9 Sheol16 below is stirred up about you,

ready to meet you when you arrive.

It rouses17 the spirits of the dead for you,

all the former leaders of the earth;18

it makes all the former kings of the nations

rise from their thrones.19

10 All of them respond to you, saying:

‘You too have become weak like us!

11 Your splendor20 has been brought down to Sheol,

as well as the sound of your stringed instruments.21

You lie on a bed of maggots,

with a blanket of worms over you.22

12 Look how you have fallen from the sky,

O shining one, son of the dawn!23

You have been cut down to the ground,

O conqueror24 of the nations!25

13 You said to yourself,26

The Lord Will Judge the Philistines

28 In the year King Ahaz died,60 this message was revealed:61

29 Don’t be so happy, all you Philistines,

just because the club that beat you has been broken!62

For a viper will grow out of the serpent’s root,

and its fruit will be a darting adder.63

30 The poor will graze in my pastures;64

the needy will rest securely.

But I will kill your root by famine;

it will put to death all your survivors.65

31 Wail, O city gate!

Cry out, O city!

Melt with fear,66 all you Philistines!

For out of the north comes a cloud of smoke,

and there are no stragglers in its ranks.67

32 How will they respond to the messengers of this nation?68

Indeed, the Lord has made Zion secure;

the oppressed among his people will find safety in her.
