The Ministry of John the Baptist

1 The beginning of the gospel1 of Jesus Christ,2 the Son of God.3 2 As it is written in Isaiah the prophet,4

“Look, I am sending my messenger ahead of you,

who will prepare your way,5

3 the voice of one shouting in the wilderness,

‘Prepare the way for the Lord,

make6 his paths straight.’”7

4 In the wilderness8 John the baptizer9 began preaching a baptism of repentance for the forgiveness of sins.10 5 People11 from the whole Judean countryside and all of Jerusalem12 were going out to him, and he was baptizing them13 in the Jordan River as they confessed their sins. 6 John wore a garment made of camel’s hair with a leather belt around his waist, and he ate locusts and wild honey.14 7 He proclaimed,15 “One more powerful than I am is coming after me; I am not worthy16 to bend down and untie the strap17 of his sandals. 8 I baptize you with water, but he will baptize you with the Holy Spirit.”

The Baptism and Temptation of Jesus

9 Now18 in those days Jesus came from Nazareth19 in Galilee and was baptized by John in the Jordan River.20 10 And just as Jesus21 was coming up out of the water, he saw the heavens22 splitting apart and the Spirit descending on him like a dove.23 11 And a voice came from heaven: “You are my one dear Son;24 in you I take great delight.”25 12 The Spirit immediately drove him into the wilderness. 13 He was in the wilderness forty days,26 enduring temptations from Satan. He27 was with wild animals, and angels were ministering to his needs.28

Preaching in Galilee and the Call of the Disciples

14 Now after John was imprisoned,29 Jesus went into Galilee and proclaimed the gospel30 of God.31 15 He32 said, “The time is fulfilled and the kingdom of God33 is near. Repent and believe the gospel!” 16 As he went along the Sea of Galilee, he saw Simon and Andrew, Simon’s brother, casting a net into the sea (for they were fishermen).34 17 Jesus said to them, “Follow me, and I will turn you into fishers of people.”35 18 They left their nets immediately and followed him.36 19 Going on a little farther, he saw James, the son of Zebedee, and John his brother in their37 boat mending nets. 20 Immediately he called them, and they left their father Zebedee in the boat with the hired men and followed him.

Jesus’ Authority

21 Then38 they went to Capernaum.39 When the Sabbath came,40 Jesus41 went into the synagogue42 and began to teach. 22 The people there43 were amazed by his teaching, because he taught them like one who had authority,44 not like the experts in the law.45 23 Just then there was a man in their synagogue with an unclean spirit,46 and he cried out,47 24 “Leave us alone,48 Jesus the Nazarene! Have you come to destroy us? I know who you are – the Holy One49 of God!” 25 But50 Jesus rebuked him:51 “Silence! Come out of him!”52 26 After throwing him into convulsions, the unclean spirit cried out with a loud voice and came out of him. 27 They were all amazed so that they asked each other, “What is this? A new teaching with authority! He even commands the unclean spirits and they obey him.” 28 So53 the news about him spread quickly throughout all the region around Galilee.

Healings at Simon’s House

29 Now54 as soon as they left the synagogue,55 they entered Simon and Andrew’s house, with James and John. 30 Simon’s mother-in-law was lying down, sick with a fever, so56 they spoke to Jesus57 at once about her. 31 He came and raised her up by gently taking her hand. Then the fever left her and she began to serve58 them. 32 When it was evening, after sunset, they brought to him all who were sick and demon-possessed. 33 The whole town gathered by the door. 34 So59 he healed many who were sick with various diseases and drove out many demons.60 But61 he would not permit the demons to speak,62 because they knew him.63

Praying and Preaching

35 Then64 Jesus65 got up early in the morning when it was still very dark, departed, and went out to a deserted place, and there he spent time in prayer.66 36 Simon and his companions searched for him. 37 When they found him, they said, “Everyone is looking for you.” 38 He replied,67 “Let us go elsewhere, into the surrounding villages, so that I can preach there too. For that is what I came out here to do.”68 39 So69 he went into all of Galilee preaching in their synagogues70 and casting out demons.

Cleansing a Leper

40 Now71 a leper72 came to him and fell to his knees, asking for help. “If73 you are willing, you can make me clean,” he said. 41 Moved with compassion,74 Jesus75 stretched out his hand and touched76 him, saying, “I am willing. Be clean!” 42 The leprosy left him at once, and he was clean. 43 Immediately Jesus77 sent the man78 away with a very strong warning. 44 He told him,79 “See that you do not say anything to anyone,80 but go, show yourself to a priest, and bring the offering that Moses commanded81 for your cleansing, as a testimony to them.”82 45 But as the man83 went out he began to announce it publicly and spread the story widely, so that Jesus84 was no longer able to enter any town openly but stayed outside in remote places. Still85 they kept coming86 to him from everywhere.
