Psalm 191

For the music director; a psalm of David.

1 The heavens declare the glory of God;2

the sky displays his handiwork.3

2 Day after day it speaks out;4

night after night it reveals his greatness.5

3 There is no actual speech or word,

nor is its6 voice literally heard.

4 Yet its voice7 echoes8 throughout the earth;

its9 words carry10 to the distant horizon.11

In the sky12 he has pitched a tent for the sun.13

5 Like a bridegroom it emerges14 from its chamber;15

like a strong man it enjoys16 running its course.17

6 It emerges from the distant horizon,18

and goes from one end of the sky to the other;19

nothing can escape20 its heat.

7 The law of the Lord is perfect

and preserves one’s life.21

The rules set down by the Lord22 are reliable23

and impart wisdom to the inexperienced.24

8 The Lord’s precepts are fair25

and make one joyful.26

The Lord’s commands27 are pure28

and give insight for life.29

9 The commands to fear the Lord are right30

and endure forever.31

The judgments given by the Lord are trustworthy

and absolutely just.32

10 They are of greater value33 than gold,

than even a great amount of pure gold;

they bring greater delight34 than honey,

than even the sweetest honey from a honeycomb.

11 Yes, your servant finds moral guidance there;35

those who obey them receive a rich reward.36

12 Who can know all his errors?37

Please do not punish me for sins I am unaware of.38

13 Moreover, keep me from committing flagrant39 sins;

do not allow such sins to control me.40

Then I will be blameless,

and innocent of blatant41 rebellion.

14 May my words and my thoughts

be acceptable in your sight,42

O Lord, my sheltering rock43 and my redeemer.44
