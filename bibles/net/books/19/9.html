Psalm 91

For the music director; according to the alumoth-labben style;2 a psalm of David.

1 I will thank the Lord with all my heart!

I will tell about all your amazing deeds!3

2 I will be happy and rejoice in you!

I will sing praises to you, O sovereign One!4

3 When my enemies turn back,

they trip and are defeated5 before you.

4 For you defended my just cause;6

from your throne you pronounced a just decision.7

5 You terrified the nations with your battle cry;8

you destroyed the wicked;9

you permanently wiped out all memory of them.10

6 The enemy’s cities have been reduced to permanent ruins;11

you destroyed their cities;12

all memory of the enemies has perished.13

7 But the Lord14 rules15 forever;

he reigns in a just manner.16

8 He judges the world fairly;

he makes just legal decisions for the nations.17

9 Consequently18 the Lord provides safety for the oppressed;19

he provides safety in times of trouble.20

10 Your loyal followers trust in you,21

for you, Lord, do not abandon those who seek your help.22

11 Sing praises to the Lord, who rules23 in Zion!

Tell the nations what he has done!24

12 For the one who takes revenge against murderers took notice of the oppressed;25

he did not overlook26 their cry for help27

13 when they prayed:28

“Have mercy on me,29 Lord!

See how I am oppressed by those who hate me,30

O one who can snatch me away31 from the gates of death!

14 Then I will32 tell about all your praiseworthy acts;33

in the gates of Daughter Zion34 I will rejoice because of your deliverance.”35

15 The nations fell36 into the pit they had made;

their feet were caught in the net they had hidden.37

16 The Lord revealed himself;

he accomplished justice;

the wicked were ensnared by their own actions.38 (Higgaion.39 Selah)

17 The wicked are turned back and sent to Sheol;40

this is the destiny of41 all the nations that ignore42 God,

18 for the needy are not permanently ignored,43

the hopes of the oppressed are not forever dashed.44

19 Rise up, Lord!45

Don’t let men be defiant!46

May the nations be judged in your presence!

20 Terrify them, Lord!47

Let the nations know they are mere mortals!48 (Selah)
