Psalm 71

A musical composition2 by David, which he sang to the Lord concerning3 a Benjaminite named Cush.4

1 O Lord my God, in you I have taken shelter.5

Deliver me from all who chase me! Rescue me!

2 Otherwise they will rip6 me7 to shreds like a lion;

they will tear me to bits and no one will be able to rescue me.8

3 O Lord my God, if I have done what they say,9

or am guilty of unjust actions,10

4 or have wronged my ally,11

or helped his lawless enemy,12

5 may an enemy relentlessly chase13 me14 and catch me;15

may he trample me to death16

and leave me lying dishonored in the dust.17 (Selah)

6 Stand up angrily,18 Lord!

Rise up with raging fury against my enemies!19

Wake up for my sake and execute the judgment you have decreed for them!20

7 The countries are assembled all around you;21

take once more your rightful place over them!22

8 The Lord judges the nations.23

Vindicate me, Lord, because I am innocent,24

because I am blameless,25 O Exalted One!26

9 May the evil deeds of the wicked27 come to an end!28

But make the innocent29 secure,30

O righteous God,

you who examine31 inner thoughts and motives!32

10 The Exalted God is my shield,33

the one who delivers the morally upright.34

11 God is a just judge;

he is angry throughout the day.35

12 If a person36 does not repent, God sharpens his sword37

and prepares to shoot his bow.38

13 He prepares to use deadly weapons against him;39

he gets ready to shoot flaming arrows.40

14 See the one who is pregnant with wickedness,

who conceives destructive plans,

and gives birth to harmful lies – 41

15 he digs a pit42

and then falls into the hole he has made.43

16 He becomes the victim of his own destructive plans44

and the violence he intended for others falls on his own head.45

17 I will thank the Lord for46 his justice;

I will sing praises to the sovereign Lord!47
