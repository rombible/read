Psalm 771

For the music director, Jeduthun; a psalm of Asaph.

1 I will cry out to God2 and call for help!

I will cry out to God and he will pay attention3 to me.

2 In my time of trouble I sought4 the Lord.

I kept my hand raised in prayer throughout the night.5

I6 refused to be comforted.

3 I said, “I will remember God while I groan;

I will think about him while my strength leaves me.”7 (Selah)

4 You held my eyelids open;8

I was troubled and could not speak.9

5 I thought about the days of old,

about ancient times.10

6 I said, “During the night I will remember the song I once sang;

I will think very carefully.”

I tried to make sense of what was happening.11

7 I asked,12 “Will the Lord reject me forever?

Will he never again show me his favor?

8 Has his loyal love disappeared forever?

Has his promise13 failed forever?

9 Has God forgotten to be merciful?

Has his anger stifled his compassion?”

10 Then I said, “I am sickened by the thought

that the sovereign One14 might become inactive.15

11 I will remember the works of the Lord.

Yes, I will remember the amazing things you did long ago!16

12 I will think about all you have done;

I will reflect upon your deeds!”

1317 O God, your deeds are extraordinary!18

What god can compare to our great God?19

14 You are the God who does amazing things;

you have revealed your strength among the nations.

15 You delivered20 your people by your strength21 –

the children of Jacob and Joseph. (Selah)

16 The waters22 saw you, O God,

the waters saw you and trembled.23

Yes, the depths of the sea24 shook with fear.25

17 The clouds poured down rain;26

the skies thundered.27

Yes, your arrows28 flashed about.

18 Your thunderous voice was heard in the wind;

the lightning bolts lit up the world;

the earth trembled and shook.29

19 You walked through the sea;30

you passed through the surging waters,31

but left no footprints.32

20 You led your people like a flock of sheep,

by the hand of Moses and Aaron.
