Psalm 551

For the music director, to be accompanied by stringed instruments; a well-written song2 by David.

1 Listen, O God, to my prayer!

Do not ignore3 my appeal for mercy!

2 Pay attention to me and answer me!

I am so upset4 and distressed,5 I am beside myself,6

3 because of what the enemy says,7

and because of how the wicked8 pressure me,9

for they hurl trouble10 down upon me11

and angrily attack me.

4 My heart beats violently12 within me;

the horrors of death overcome me.13

5 Fear and panic overpower me;14

terror overwhelms15 me.

6 I say,16 “I wish I had wings like a dove!

I would fly away and settle in a safe place!

7 Look, I will escape to a distant place;

I will stay in the wilderness. (Selah)

8 I will hurry off to a place that is safe

from the strong wind17 and the gale.”

9 Confuse them,18 O Lord!

Frustrate their plans!19

For I see violence and conflict in the city.

10 Day and night they walk around on its walls,20

while wickedness and destruction21 are within it.

11 Disaster is within it;

violence22 and deceit do not depart from its public square.

12 Indeed,23 it is not an enemy who insults me,

or else I could bear it;

it is not one who hates me who arrogantly taunts me,24

or else I could hide from him.

13 But it is you,25 a man like me,26

my close friend in whom I confided.27

14 We would share personal thoughts with each other;28

in God’s temple we would walk together among the crowd.

15 May death destroy them!29

May they go down alive into Sheol!30

For evil is in their dwelling place and in their midst.

16 As for me, I will call out to God,

and the Lord will deliver me.

17 During the evening, morning, and noontime

I will lament and moan,31

and he will hear32 me.33

18 He will rescue34 me and protect me from those who attack me,35

even though36 they greatly outnumber me.37

19 God, the one who has reigned as king from long ago,

will hear and humiliate them.38 (Selah)

They refuse to change,

and do not fear God.39

20 He40 attacks41 his friends;42

he breaks his solemn promises to them.43

21 His words are as smooth as butter,44

but he harbors animosity in his heart.45

His words seem softer than oil,

but they are really like sharp swords.46

22 Throw your burden47 upon the Lord,

and he will sustain you.48

He will never allow the godly to be upended.49

23 But you, O God, will bring them50 down to the deep Pit.51

Violent and deceitful people52 will not live even half a normal lifespan.53

But as for me, I trust in you.
