Psalm 831

A song, a psalm of Asaph.

1 O God, do not be silent!

Do not ignore us!2 Do not be inactive, O God!

2 For look, your enemies are making a commotion;

those who hate you are hostile.3

3 They carefully plot4 against your people,

and make plans to harm5 the ones you cherish.6

4 They say, “Come on, let’s annihilate them so they are no longer a nation!7

Then the name of Israel will be remembered no more.”

5 Yes,8 they devise a unified strategy;9

they form an alliance10 against you.

6 It includes11 the tents of Edom and the Ishmaelites,

Moab and the Hagrites,12

7 Gebal,13 Ammon, and Amalek,

Philistia and the inhabitants of Tyre.14

8 Even Assyria has allied with them,

lending its strength to the descendants of Lot.15 (Selah)

9 Do to them as you did to Midian16 –

as you did to Sisera and Jabin at the Kishon River!17

10 They were destroyed at Endor;18

their corpses were like manure19 on the ground.

11 Make their nobles like Oreb and Zeeb,20

and all their rulers like Zebah and Zalmunna,21

12 who said,22 “Let’s take over23 the pastures of God!”

13 O my God, make them like dead thistles,24

like dead weeds blown away by25 the wind!

14 Like the fire that burns down the forest,

or the flames that consume the mountainsides,26

15 chase them with your gale winds,

and terrify27 them with your windstorm.

16 Cover28 their faces with shame,

so they might seek29 you,30 O Lord.

17 May they be humiliated and continually terrified!31

May they die in shame!32

18 Then they will know33 that you alone are the Lord,34

the sovereign king35 over all the earth.
