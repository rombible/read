The Second Blow: Frogs

251 Seven full days passed2 after the Lord struck3 the Nile. 1 (7:26)4 Then the Lord said to Moses, “Go to Pharaoh and tell him, ‘Thus says the Lord: “Release my people in order that they may serve me! 2 But if you refuse to release them, then I am going to plague5 all your territory with frogs.6 3 The Nile will swarm7 with frogs, and they will come up and go into your house, in your bedroom, and on your bed, and into the houses of your servants and your people, and into your ovens and your kneading troughs.8 4 Frogs9 will come up against you, your people, and all your servants.”’”10

5 The Lord spoke to Moses, “Tell Aaron, ‘Extend your hand with your staff11 over the rivers, over the canals, and over the ponds, and bring the frogs up over the land of Egypt.’” 6 So Aaron extended his hand over the waters of Egypt, and frogs12 came up and covered the land of Egypt.

7 The magicians did the same13 with their secret arts and brought up frogs on the land of Egypt too.14

8 Then Pharaoh summoned15 Moses and Aaron and said, “Pray16 to the Lord that he may take the frogs away17 from me and my people, and I will release18 the people that they may sacrifice19 to the Lord.” 9 Moses said to Pharaoh, “You may have the honor over me20 – when shall I pray for you, your servants, and your people, for the frogs to be removed21 from you and your houses, so that22 they will be left23 only in the Nile?” 10 He said, “Tomorrow.” And Moses said,24 “It will be25 as you say,26 so that you may know that there is no one like the Lord our God. 11 The frogs will depart from you, your houses, your servants, and your people; they will be left only in the Nile.”

12 Then Moses and Aaron went out from Pharaoh, and Moses cried27 to the Lord because of28 the frogs that he had brought on29 Pharaoh. 13 The Lord did as Moses asked30 – the31 frogs died out of the houses, the villages, and the fields. 14 The Egyptians32 piled them in countless heaps,33 and the land stank. 15 But when Pharaoh saw that there was relief,34 he hardened35 his heart and did not listen to them, just as the Lord had predicted.36

The Third Blow: Gnats

1637 The Lord said to Moses, “Tell Aaron, ‘Extend your staff and strike the dust of the ground, and it will become38 gnats39 throughout all the land of Egypt.’” 17 They did so; Aaron extended his hand with his staff, he struck the dust of the ground, and it became gnats on people40 and on animals. All the dust of the ground became gnats throughout all the land of Egypt. 18 When41 the magicians attempted42 to bring forth gnats by their secret arts, they could not. So there were gnats on people and on animals. 19 The magicians said43 to Pharaoh, “It is the finger44 of God!” But Pharaoh’s heart remained hard,45 and he did not listen to them, just as the Lord had predicted.

The Fourth Blow: Flies

2046 The Lord47 said to Moses, “Get up early in the morning and position yourself before Pharaoh as he goes out to the water, and tell him, ‘Thus says the Lord, “Release my people that they may serve me! 21 If you do not release48 my people, then I am going to send49 swarms of flies50 on you and on your servants and on your people and in your houses. The houses of the Egyptians will be full of flies, and even the ground they stand on.51 22 But on that day I will mark off52 the land of Goshen, where my people are staying,53 so that no swarms of flies will be there, that you may know that I am the Lord in the midst of this land.54 23 I will put a division55 between my people and your people. This sign will take place56 tomorrow.”’” 24 The Lord did so; a57 thick58 swarm of flies came into59 Pharaoh’s house and into the houses60 of his servants, and throughout the whole land of Egypt the land was ruined61 because of the swarms of flies.

25 Then Pharaoh summoned Moses and Aaron and said, “Go, sacrifice to your God within the land.”62 26 But Moses said, “That would not be the right thing to do,63 for the sacrifices we make64 to the Lord our God would be an abomination65 to the Egyptians.66 If we make sacrifices that are an abomination to the Egyptians right before their eyes,67 will they not stone us?68 27 We must go69 on a three-day journey70 into the desert and sacrifice71 to the Lord our God, just as he is telling us.”72

28 Pharaoh said, “I will release you73 so that you may sacrifice74 to the Lord your God in the desert. Only you must not go very far.75 Do76 pray for me.”

29 Moses said, “I am going to go out77 from you and pray to the Lord, and the swarms of flies will go away from Pharaoh, from his servants, and from his people tomorrow. Only do not let Pharaoh deal falsely again78 by not releasing79 the people to sacrifice to the Lord.” 30 So Moses went out from Pharaoh and prayed to the Lord, 31 and the Lord did as Moses asked80 – he removed the swarms of flies from Pharaoh, from his servants, and from his people. Not one remained! 32 But Pharaoh hardened81 his heart this time also and did not release the people.
