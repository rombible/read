1 Every wise woman1 builds2 her household,3

but a foolish woman tears it down with her own hands.

2 The one who walks in his uprightness fears the Lord,4

but the one who is perverted in his ways5 despises him.

3 In6 the speech7 of a fool is a rod for his back,8

but the words9 of the wise protect them.

4 Where there are no oxen, the feeding trough is clean,

but an abundant harvest is produced by strong oxen.10

5 A truthful witness11 does not lie,

but a false witness12 breathes out lies.13

6 The scorner14 seeks wisdom but finds none,15

but understanding is easy16 for a discerning person.

7 Leave the presence of a foolish person,17

or18 you will not understand19 wise counsel.20

8 The wisdom of the shrewd person21 is to discern22 his way,

but the folly of fools is deception.23

9 Fools mock24 at reparation,25

but among the upright there is favor.26

10 The heart knows its own bitterness,27

and with its joy no one else28 can share.29

11 The household30 of the wicked will be destroyed,

but the tent31 of the upright will flourish.

12 There is a way that seems right to a person,32

but its end is the way that leads to death.33

13 Even in laughter the heart may ache,34

and the end35 of joy may be36 grief.

14 The backslider37 will be paid back38 from his own ways,

but a good person will be rewarded39 for his.

15 A naive person40 believes everything,

but the shrewd person discerns his steps.41

16 A wise person is cautious42 and turns from evil,

but a fool throws off restraint43 and is overconfident.44

17 A person who has a quick temper45 does foolish things,

and a person with crafty schemes46 is hated.47

18 The naive inherit48 folly,

but the shrewd49 are crowned50 with knowledge.

19 Those who are evil will bow51 before those who are good,

and the wicked will bow52 at the gates53 of the righteous.

20 A poor person is disliked54 even by his neighbors,

but those who love the rich are many.

21 The one who despises his neighbor sins,

but whoever is kind to the needy is blessed.

22 Do not those who devise55 evil go astray?

But those who plan good exhibit56 faithful covenant love.57

23 In all hard work58 there is profit,

but merely talking about it59 only brings60 poverty.61

24 The crown of the wise is their riches,62

but the folly63 of fools is folly.

25 A truthful witness64 rescues lives,65

but the one who breathes lies brings66 deception.67

26 In the fear of the Lord one has68 strong confidence,69

and it will be a refuge70 for his children.

27 The fear of the Lord71 is like72 a life-giving fountain,73

to turn74 people75 from deadly snares.76

28 A king’s glory is77 the abundance of people,

but the lack of subjects78 is the ruin79 of a ruler.

29 The one who is slow to anger has great understanding,

but the one who has a quick temper80 exalts81 folly.

30 A tranquil spirit82 revives the body,83

but envy84 is rottenness to the bones.85

31 The one who oppresses86 the poor insults87 his Creator,

but whoever shows favor88 to the needy honors him.

32 The wicked will be thrown down in his trouble,89

but the righteous have refuge90 even in the threat of death.91

33 Wisdom rests in the heart of the discerning;

it is known92 even in the heart93 of fools.

34 Righteousness exalts94 a nation,

but sin is a disgrace95 to any people.

35 The king shows favor96 to a wise97 servant,

but his wrath falls98 on one who acts shamefully.
