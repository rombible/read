Strong's Concordance
eden: a base, pedestal
Original Word: אֶדֶן
Part of Speech: Noun Masculine
Transliteration: eden
Phonetic Spelling: (eh'-den)
Definition: a base, pedestal
NAS Exhaustive Concordance
Word Origin
from the same as adon
Definition
a base, pedestal
NASB Translation
bases (1), pedestals (1), socket (1), sockets (52).

Brown-Driver-Briggs
[אֶ֫דֶן]57 noun masculineExodus 26:19 base, pedestal — אָ֑דֶן Exodus 38:7; plural אֲדָנִים Exodus 26:19 +; construct אַדְנֵי Songs 5:15 +, etc.

1 pedestals of fine gold, on which pillars of marble were set Songs 5:15.

2 pedestals of the earth on which its pillars were placed Job 38:6 ("" corner-stone).

3 (metal) pedestals, bases, or sockets in which tenons of planks & pillars of tabernacle were set up; two for each plank & one for each pillar Exodus 26:19 (3 t. in verse) + 52 t. in Exodus 26, 27, 35-40 Numbers 3, 4 (all P); compare ᵐ5 Sm Co for אָרְכּוֺ Ezekiel 41:22, of altar.

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance

From the same as 'adown (in the sense of strength); a basis (of a building, a column, etc.) -- foundation, socket.
