Brown-Driver-Briggs
II. [מָּרָשׁ] noun [masculine] horse, steed (less common synonym of סוּס ( > explained away by SchwZAW viii (1888). 191); Arabic horse, mare (often), so Ethiopic : compare Sabean פרס horse, MordtmHim. Inschr. 70); — Plural מָּרָשִׁים (erroneous for * מְּרָשִׁים Köii. 1, 89) Ezekiel 27:14 +, suffix מָּרָשָׁיו Isaiah 28:28; 1 Samuel 8:11; — steeds Ezekiel 27:14 ( + סוּסִים, מְּרָדִים), from Togarmah; simile of swiftness Joel 2:4 ("" סוּסִים); probably also Isaiah 28:28; Jeremiah 46:4 ("" סוּסִים, compare Gf Gie), and perhaps ׳וְשָׂם לוֺ בְּמֶרְכַבְחוֺ וּבָעָרָשָׁיו וְרָצוּ וגו 1 Samuel 8:11 (king subject).

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance
horseman

From parash; a steed (as stretched out to a vehicle, not single nor for mounting (compare cuwc)); also (by implication) a driver (in a chariot), i.e. (collectively) cavalry -- horseman.
