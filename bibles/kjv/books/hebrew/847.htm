◄ 847. Eshta'ol ►
Strong's Concordance
Eshta'ol: Eshtaol
Original Word: אֶשְׁתָּאֹל
Part of Speech: Proper Name Location
Transliteration: Eshta'ol
Phonetic Spelling: (esh-taw-ole')
Definition: to found, establish
Brown-Driver-Briggs
אֶשְׁתָּאוֺל Joshua 15:33 2t., אֶשְׁתָּאֹל Judges 13:25 3t. proper name, of a location (in form like the infinitive of the Arabic VIII. conjugation from שָׁאַל: so אֶשְׁתְּמֹעַ from שָׁמַע. Perhaps Arabic-speaking tribes may have settled in parts of south of Judah) city of Danites in the שְׁפֵלָה of Judah, named with צָרְעָה Joshua 15:33; Joshua 19:41; Judges 13:25; Judges 16:31; Judges 18:2,8,11; perhaps modern Eshû±­ Surveyiii. 25 GuérinPal. ii. 13 f.382

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance
Eshtaol

Or meshtafowl {esh-taw-ole'}; probably from sha'al; intreaty; Eshtaol, a place in Palestine -- Eshtaol.

see HEBREW sha'al
