emthan: terrible

NAS Exhaustive Concordance
Word Origin
the same as emethan, q.v.

Brown-Driver-Briggs
[אֵימְתָן D§ 35, 1 θ]

adjective terrible (ᵑ7 אֵימְתָן, from אֵימְתָא = Biblical Hebrew אֵימָה, q. v. v אים); — feminine singular absolute אֵימְתָנִי (K§ 61, 5)) Daniel 7:7 (on form see PrätZMG lvi (1902), 156 NöBSSW 51, Anm. 3).

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance
terrible

(Aramaic) from a root corresponding to that of mothen; well-loined (i.e. Burly) or mighty -- terrible.

see HEBREW mothen
