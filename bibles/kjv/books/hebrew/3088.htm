Brown-Driver-Briggs
יְהוֺרָם, יוֺרָם, יֹרָם

proper name, masculine (׳י is exalted; compare מַלְכִּירָם; further, Phoenician בעלרם, רמבעל; Sabean אלרם DHMZMG 1876, 686) —

1 ׳יְהוֺ (ᵐ5 Ἰωραμ) king of Judah, son of Jehoshaphat 1 Kings 22:51; 2 Kings 1:17; 2 Kings 8:16,25,29; 2 Kings 12:19; 2Chronicles 21:1,3,4,5,9,16; 22:1,6,11; = ׳יוֺ 2 Kings 8:21,23,24; 2 Kings 11:2; 1 Chronicles 3:11.

2 ׳יְהוֺ (ᵐ5 Ἰωραμ) king of Israel son of Ahab 2 Kings 1:17; 2 Kings 3:1,6; 2 Kings 9:15,17,21 (twice in verse); 2 Kings 9:22,23; 2Chronicles 22:5,6,7; = ׳יוֺ 2 Kings 8:16,25,28 (twice in verse); 2 Kings 8:29 (twice in verse); 2 Kings 9:14 (twice in verse); 2 Kings 9:16 (twice in verse); 2 Kings 9:29; 2Chronicles 22:5,7.

3 ׳יְהוֺpriest in time of Jehoshaphat 2 Chronicles 17:8 (ᵐ5 Ἰωραμ, Ἰωραν).

4 ׳יוֺ son of Tou, king of Hamath 2 Samuel 8:10 (ᵐ5 Ἰεδδουραν) probably error for הֲדוֺרָם "" 1 Chronicles 18:10.

5 יֹרָםa Levite 1 Chronicles 26:25 (ᵐ5 Ἰωραμ).

Jehoram, Joram

From Yhovah and ruwm; Jehovah-raised; Jehoram, the name of a Syrian and of three Israelites -- Jehoram, Joram. Compare Yowram.
