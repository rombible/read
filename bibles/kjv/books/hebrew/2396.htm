Chizqiyyah or Chizqiyyahu or Yechizqiyyah or Yechizqiyyahu: "Yah has strengthened," a king of Judah, also several other Isr.
Original Word: חִזְקִיָּה
Part of Speech: Proper Name Masculine
Transliteration: Chizqiyyah or Chizqiyyahu or Yechizqiyyah or Yechizqiyyahu
Phonetic Spelling: (khiz-kee-yaw')
Definition: "Yah has strengthened", a king of Judah, also several other Isr

Word Origin

Definition
"Yah has strengthened," a king of Judah, also several other Isr.

Brown-Driver-Briggs
חִזְקִיָּ֫הוּ, חִזְקִיָּה, יְחִזְקִיָּהוּ, יְחִזְקִיָּה

proper name, masculine (׳י hath strengthened, ׳י stengtheneth; = Assyrian –aza‡i(i)au, SchrCOT on 2 Kings 18:1ff., JägerBAS i, 469; compare proper name יחזק on seal GannJAs 1883 Feb. — Mar., 134 No. 7; — on formative see LagBN 134) —

1 ᵐ5 Εξεκιας, son of Ahaz, King of Judah, seldom חִזְקִיָּה 2 Kings 18:1,10,14 (twice in verse); 2 Kings 18:15,16 (twice in verse), also ׳אַנְשֵׁי ח Proverbs 25:1 ; elsewhere in 2Kings (except Proverbs 20:10) חִזְקִיָּהוּ: Proverbs 16:20; Proverbs 18:9 34t., 2 Kings 18-21 "" Isaiah 36:1 31t., Isaiah 36-39; also Jeremiah 26:18,19; 1 Chronicles 3:13; 2Chronicles 29:18,27; 30:24; 32:15; in Chronicles use. יְחִזְקִיָּחוּ 1 Chronicles 4:41; 2Chronicles 28:27 35t. 2 Chron 29-33; also 2 Kings 20:10; Jeremiah 15:4, and in title Isaiah 1:1; יִחִזְקִיָה Hosea 1:1; Micah 1:1 (if י in these three be not text error for וְ).

2 חִזְקִיָּה great-great-grandfather of prophet Zephaniah Zephaniah 1:1, ᵐ5 τοῦ Εξεκιου, perhaps =

1

3 ᵐ5 Εξεκια(ς), חִזְקִיָּה man of royal Davidic line 1 Chronicles 3:23 probably near time of Char.

4 יְחִזְקִיָּה, ᵐ5 Εξεκια(ς), head of a family of returned exiles Ezra 2:16 = חִזְקִיָּה Nehemiah 7:21, compare also ׳ח Nehemiah 10:18.

5 יְחִזְקִיָּהוּ ᵐ5 Εξεκιας, an Ephraimite, Ahaz's time 2 Chronicles 28:12.



Strong's Exhaustive Concordance
Hezekiah, Hizkiah, Hizkijah

Or Chizqiyahuw {khiz-kee-yaw'-hoo}; also Ychizqiyah {yekh-iz-kee-yaw'}; or Ychizqiyahuw {yekh-iz-kee-yaw'-hoo}; from chazaq and Yahh; strengthened of Jah; Chizkijah, a king of Judah, also the name of two other Israelites -- Hezekiah, Hizkiah, Hizkijah. Compare Ychizqiyah.
