Brown-Driver-Briggs
I. מְּלֹנִי pronoun a certain one; Greek ὁ, ἡ δεῖνα (according to Thes from above √, through an unused noun מָּלֹן, properly one defined, a particular one: Late Hebrew id.; Aramaic , feminine ; Arabic WAG i.§ 353 R.c, voc. SiegfZW Th xxvii. 355; As pulpul HptBAS i. 114), always joined with צַלְמֹנִי (properly one not mentioned) in the sense of 'ein gewisser verschwiegener' (Ew§ 106 c, 2 Sta§ 150 b), 'a certain unnamed one', i.e. such a one; Ruth 4:1 שְׁבָהמֹּֿה מְּלֹנִי אַלְמֹנִי sit down here, such a one (Greek ὦ οὖτος; Arabic ); in the phrase ׳א ׳מְקוֺם פ the place of such a one = such and such a place, 1 Samuel 21:3; 2 Kings 6:8. From ׳א ׳פ arose by contraction (or conflation, PerlesAnal.82) מַּלְמֹנִי Daniel 8:13 ויאמר ֗֗֗ הַמְדַבֵּר ׳לַמּ to that certain onewho spake. — II.
