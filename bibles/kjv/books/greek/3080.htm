Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 3080: λύσις

λύσις, λυσεως, ἡ (λύω) (from Homer down), a loosing of any bond, as that of marriage; hence, once in the N. T. of divorce, 1 Corinthians 7:27.

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance

From luo; a loosening, i.e. (specially), divorce -- to be loosed.
