Strong's Concordance
agnoia: ignorance
Original Word: ἄγνοια, ας, ἡ
Part of Speech: Noun, Feminine
Transliteration: agnoia
Phonetic Spelling: (ag'-noy-ah)
Definition: ignorance
Usage: ignorance, inadvertence, sometimes with the idea of willful blindness.

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 52: ἄγνοια

ἄγνοια, (ας, ἡ (from Aeschylus down), want of knowledge, ignorance, especially of divine things: Acts 17:30; 1 Peter 1:14; such as is inexcusable, Ephesians 4:18 (Wis. 14:22); of moral blindness, Acts 3:17. (Cf. ἀγνοέω.)
