Strong's Concordance
opheilé: a debt
Original Word: ὀφειλή, ῆς, ἡ
Part of Speech: Noun, Feminine
Transliteration: opheilé
Phonetic Spelling: (of-i-lay')
Definition: a debt
Usage: a debt, a duty, what is due.
HELPS Word-studies

Cognate: 3782 opheilḗ (a feminine noun) – a specific (applied) kind of indebtedness, implying an "applied obligation" due to the debt (what is owed). See 3781 (opheiletēs).

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 3782: ὀφειλή

ὀφειλή, ὀφειλης, ἡ (ὀφείλω), that which is owed; properly, a debt: Matthew 18:32; metaphorical plural, dues: Romans 13:7; specifically, of conjugal duty (R. V. her due), 1 Corinthians 7:3 G L T Tr WH. Found neither in the Greek O. T. nor in secular authors; cf. Lob. ad Phryn., p. 90.
