Rhómaios: Roman
Original Word: Ῥωμαῖος, α, ον
Part of Speech: Adjective
Transliteration: Rhómaios
Phonetic Spelling: (hro-mah'-yos)
Definition: Roman
Usage: Roman; subst: a Roman citizen.

Word Origin
from Rhómé
Definition
Roman
NASB Translation
Roman (5), Romans (6), Rome (1).

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 4514: Ῥωμαῖος

Ῥωμαῖος, Ρ᾽ωμαιου, ὁ, a Roman: John 11:48; Acts 2:10 (R. V. here from Rome); Acts 16:21, 37; Acts 22:25-27, 29; Acts 23:27; Acts 25:16; Acts 28:17. ((Polybius, Josephus, others); often in 1 and 2 Macc.)

From Rhome; Romaean, i.e. Roman (as noun) -- Roman, of Rome.
