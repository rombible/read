Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 962: Βηθαβαρᾶ

Βηθαβαρᾶ, Βηθαβαρας Βηθαβαρᾶ Rec.bez st, indeclinable), ἡ (עֲבָרָה בֵּית place of crossing, i. e. where there is a crossing or ford, cf. German Furthhausen), Bethabara: John 1:28 Rec. (in Rec.elz of 1st decl., but cf. Winers Grammar, 61 (60)); see (WH's Appendix at the passage and) Βηθανία, 2.

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance

Of Hebrew origin (bayith and abarah); ferry-house; Bethabara (i.e. Bethabarah), a place on the Jordan -- Bethabara.
