Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 3632: οἰνοφλυγία

οἰνοφλυγία, ὀινοφλυγιας, ἡ (οἰνοφλυγέω, and this from οἰνόφλυξ, which is compounded of οἶνος and φλύω, to bubble up, overflow), drunkenness (A. V. wine-bibbing): 1 Peter 4:3. (Xenophon, oec. 1, 22; Aristotle, eth. Nic. 3, 6, 15; Polybius 2, 19, 4; Philo, vita Moys. iii., § 22 (for other examples see Siegfried, Philo etc., p. 102); Aelian v. h. 3, 14.) (Cf. Trench, § lxi.)
