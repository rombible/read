Strong's Concordance
prótostatés: one who stands first (of soldiers), hence a leader
Original Word: πρωτοστάτης, ου, ὁ
Part of Speech: Noun, Masculine
Transliteration: prótostatés
Phonetic Spelling: (pro-tos-tat'-ace)
Definition: one who stands first (of soldiers), a leader
Usage: one who stands in the front rank, hence: a leader, ringleader, chief.

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 4414: πρωτοστάτης

πρωτοστάτης, πρωτοστατου, ὁ (πρῶτος and ἵστημι), properly, one who stands in the front rank, a front-rank man, (Thucydides, Xenophon, Polybius, Diodorus, Dionysius Halicarnassus, others; ὥσπερ στρατηγός πρωτοστάτης, Job 15:24); hence, a leader, chief, champion: tropically, (A. V. a ringleader) τῆς αἱρέσεως, Acts 24
