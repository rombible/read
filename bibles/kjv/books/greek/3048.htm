◄ 3048. logeia ►
Strong's Concordance
logeia: a collection
Original Word: λογία, ας, ἡ
Part of Speech: Noun, Feminine
Transliteration: logeia
Phonetic Spelling: (log-ee'-ah)
Definition: a collection
Usage: a collection, collecting (of money), particularly of an irregular local contribution for religious purposes.
NAS Exhaustive Concordance
Word Origin
from logeuó (to collect)
Definition
a collection
NASB Translation
collection (1), collections (1).

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 3048: λόγια

λόγια, λογίας, ἡ (from λέγω to collect) (Vulg.collecta), a collection: of money gathered for the relief of the poor, 1 Corinthians 16:1f (Not found in secular authors (cf. Winer's Grammar, 25).)

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance
collection, gathering.

From logos (in the commercial sense); a contribution -- collection, gathering.
