Strong's Concordance
Akulas: Aquila, a Christian
Original Word: Ἀκύλας, ου, ὁ
Part of Speech: Noun, Masculine
Transliteration: Akulas
Phonetic Spelling: (ak-oo'-las)
Definition: Aquila, a Christian
Usage: the Greek way of writing the Latin Aquila, a male proper name; the husband of Priscilla (Prisca), and a Jew, of a family belonging to (Sinope in ?) Pontus.

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 207: Ἀκύλας

Ἀκύλας, (but no genitive seems to be extant, see Buttmann, 20 (18)), ὁ, Aquila, a Jew of Pontus, a tent-maker, convert to Christ, companion and ally of Paul in propagating the Christian religion: Acts 18:2, 18, 26; Romans 16:3; 1 Corinthians 16:19; 2 Timothy 4:19; (see B. D.).
