Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 1836: ἑξῆς

ἑξῆς, adverb (from ἔχω, future ἕξω; cf. έ᾿χομαι τίνος to cleave to, come next to, a thing), successively, in order, (from Homer down); ὁ, ἡ, τό ἑξῆς, the next following, the next in succession: so ἡ ἑξῆς ἡμέρα, Luke 9:37; elliptically ἐν τῇ ἑξῆς, namely, ἡμέρα, Luke 7:11 (here WH text Tr text L marginal reading ἐν τῷ ἑξῆς namely, χρόνῳ, soon afterward); τῇ ἑξῆς, namely, ἡμέρα, Acts 21:1; Acts 25:17; Acts 27:18.

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance
after, following, tomorrow, next.

From echo (in the sense of taking hold of, i.e. Adjoining); successive -- after, following, X morrow, next.
