Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 4480: ῤέδη

ῤέδη (others, ῥεδα; on the first vowel cf. Tdf.s note on Revelation as below; WHs Appendix, p. 151{a}) (according to Quintilian 1, 5, 57 (cf. 68) a Gallic word (cf. Vanicek, Fremdwörter, under the word ῥεδα)), ῥεδης, ἡ, a chariot, a species of vehicle having four wheels (Isidorus Hispal. orig. 20, 12 (sec. 511) (cf. Rich, Dict. of Antiq. under the word Rheda)): Revelation 18:13.
