taphos: a burial, hence a grave
Original Word: τάφος, ου, ὁ
Part of Speech: Noun, Masculine
Transliteration: taphos
Phonetic Spelling: (taf'-os)
Definition: a burial, a grave
Usage: a burial-place, sepulcher, tomb, grave.
NAS Exhaustive Concordance

Definition

NASB Translation
grave (5), tombs (2).

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 5028: τάφος

τάφος, τάφου, ὁ (θάπτω);

1. burial (so from Homer down).

2. a grave, sepulchre (so from Hesiod down): Matthew 23:27, 29; Matthew 27:61, 64, 66; Matthew 28:1; in a comparison: τάφος ἀνεῳγμένος ὁ λάρυγξ αὐτῶν, their speech threatens destruction to others, it is death to someone whenever they open their mouth, Romans 3:13. The Sept. for קֶבֶר; and sometimes for קְבוּרָה.
