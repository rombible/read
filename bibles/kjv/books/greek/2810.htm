Strong's Concordance
Kleopas: Cleopas, a Christian
Original Word: Κλεοπᾶς, ᾶ, ὁ
Part of Speech: Noun, Masculine
Transliteration: Kleopas
Phonetic Spelling: (kleh-op'-as)
Definition: Cleopas, a Christian
Usage: Cleopas, one of the two companions of the risen Jesus from Jerusalem to Emmaus.

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 2810: Κλεοπᾶς

Κλεοπᾶς (on the decl. cf. Buttmann, 20 (18)) (apparently contracted from Κλεοπατρος, see Ἀντιπᾶς (cf. Letronne in the Revue Archeologique, 1844-45, i., p. 485ff)), ὁ, Cleopas, one of Christ's disciples: Luke 24:18. (Cf. Lightfoot, Commentary on Galatians, p. 267; B. D. under Cleopas.)
