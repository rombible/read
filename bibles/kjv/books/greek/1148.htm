Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 1148: Δαλμανουθά

Δαλμανουθά (on the accent cf. Tdf. Proleg., p. 103), ἡ, Dalmanutha, the name of a little town or village not far from Magdala (better Magadan (which see)), or lying within its territory: Mark 8:10 (cf. Matthew 15:39), see Fritzsche at the passage (B. D. American edition under the word). Derivation of the name uncertain; cf. Keim, ii. 528 ((English translation 4:238), who associates it with Zalmonah, Numbers 33:41f, but mentions other opinions. Furrer in the Zeitschr. des Deutsch. Palaestin.-Vereins for 1879, p. 58ff identifies it with Minyeh (abbrev. Manutha, Latinmensa)).
