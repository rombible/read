Strong's Concordance
Elamités: an Elamite, an inhabitant of Elam
Original Word: Ἐλαμίτης, ου, ὁ
Part of Speech: Noun, Masculine
Transliteration: Elamités
Phonetic Spelling: (el-am-ee'-tace)
Definition: an Elamite, an inhabitant of Elam
Usage: an Elamite, one of a people living to the north of the Persian Gulf in the southern part of Persia.

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 1639: Ἐλαμίτης

Ἐλαμίτης (T WH Ἐλαμειτης (see under the word εἰ, ἰ)), Ἐλαμιτου, ὁ, an Elamite, i. e. an inhabitant of the province of Elymais, a region stretching southward to the Persian Gulf, but the boundaries of which are variously given (cf. Winers RWB under the word Elam; Vaihinger in Herzog iii., p. 747ff; Dillmann in Schenkel ii., p. 91f; Schrader in Rheim, p. 358f; Grimm on 1 Macc. 6:1; (BB. DD., see under the words, , )): Acts 2:9. (Isaiah 21:2; in Greek writings Ἐλυμαῖος, and so Judith 1:6.)
