Strong's Concordance
Kaisar: Caesar, a Roman emperor
Original Word: Καῖσαρ, αρος, ὁ
Part of Speech: Noun, Masculine
Transliteration: Kaisar
Phonetic Spelling: (kah'-ee-sar)
Definition: Caesar, a Roman emperor
Usage: Caesar, a surname of the gens Iulia, which became practically synonymous with the Emperor for the time being; in the Gospels it always refers to Tiberias.

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 2541: Καῖσαρ

Καῖσαρ, Καίσαρος (Alexander Buttmann (1873) 16 (15)), ὁ, Caesar (properly, the surname of Julius Caesar, which being adopted by Octavianus Augustus and his successors afterward became an appellative, and was appropriated by the Roman emperors as a part of their title (cf. Dict. of Biogr. and Mythol. under the word )): Matthew 22:17, 21; Mark 12:14, 16; Luke 2:1; Luke 3:1; Luke 20:22; Luke 23:2; John 19:12; Acts 11:28 (Rec.); , etc.; Philippians 4:22.
