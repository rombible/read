Strong's Concordance
horkómosia: affirmation on oath
Original Word: ὁρκωμοσία, ας, ἡ
Part of Speech: Noun, Feminine
Transliteration: horkómosia
Phonetic Spelling: (hor-ko-mos-ee'ah)
Definition: affirmation on oath
Usage: the taking of an oath, an oath.
NAS Exhaustive Concordance
Word Origin
from horkoó (to make (one) swear)
Definition
affirmation on oath
NASB Translation
oath (4).

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 3728: ὁρκωμοσία

ὁρκωμοσία, ὁρκωμοσίας, ἡ (ὁρκωματέω (ὅρκος and ὄμνυμι); cf. ἀπωμοσια, ἀντωμοσία), affirmation made on oath, the taking of an oath, an oath: Hebrews 7:20(21),21,28. (Ezekiel 17:18; 1 Esdr. 8:90 (92); Josephus, Antiquities 16, 6, 2. Cf. Delitzsch, Commentary on Hebrew, the passage cited.)
