Strong's Concordance
epithumétés: one who desires
Original Word: ἐπιθυμητής, οῦ, ὁ
Part of Speech: Noun, Masculine
Transliteration: epithumétés
Phonetic Spelling: (ep-ee-thoo-may-tace')
Definition: one who desires
Usage: an eager desirer of.
HELPS Word-studies

Cognate: 1938 epithymētḗs – lust (literally, "focused passion"). 1938 /epithymētḗs ("crave") describes passion that builds on (Gk epi, "upon") what is contrary to the will of God (used only in 1 Cor 10:6). See 1937 (epithymeō).

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 1938: ἐπιθυμητής

ἐπιθυμητής, ἐπιθυμητου, ὁ (ἐπιθυμέω), one who longs for, a craver, lover, one eager for: κακῶν, 1 Corinthians 10:6 (Numbers 11:4). In Greek writings from Herodotus down.
