Strong's Concordance
olunthos: an unripe fig
Original Word: ὄλυνθος, ου, ὁ
Part of Speech: Noun, Masculine
Transliteration: olunthos
Phonetic Spelling: (ol'-oon-thos)
Definition: an unripe fig
Usage: an unripe fig, one which, not ripening in due time, grows through the winter and falls off in the spring.

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 3653: ὄλυνθος

ὄλυνθος, ὀλυνθου, ὁ, an unripe fig (Latingrossus), which grows during the winter, yet does not come to maturity but fails off in the spring (cf. B. D. under the word ): Revelation 6:13. (Hesiod from 14; Herodotus 1, 193; Dioscorid. 1, 185; Theophrastus, caus. plant. 5, 9, 12; the Sept. Song of Solomon 2:13.)
