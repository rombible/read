Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 825: Ἀττάλεια

Ἀττάλεια (Ἀτταλλια T WH (see Iota)), Ἀτταλειας, ἡ, "Attalia], a maritime city of Pamphylia in Asia, very near the borders of Lycia, built and named by Attalus Philadelphus, king of Pergamum; now Antali (or Adalia; cf. Dict. of Geog.): Acts 14:25.
