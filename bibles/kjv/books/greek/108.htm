Azótos: Azotus (i.e. Ashdod), a Philistine city
Original Word: Ἄζωτος, ου, ἡ
Part of Speech: Noun, Feminine
Transliteration: Azótos
Phonetic Spelling: (ad'-zo-tos)
Definition: Azotus (Ashdod), a Philistine city
Usage: Azotus, Ashdod, a coast town of Palestine belonging to the ancient Philistia, and part of Herod's kingdom.

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 108: Ἄζωτος

Ἄζωτος, , ἡ, אַשְׁדֹּד, Azotus, Ashdod, one of the five chief cities of the Philistines, lying between Ashkelon and Jamnia (i. e. Jabneel) and near the Mediterranean: Acts 8:40; at present a petty village, Esdud. A succinct history of the city is given by Gesenius, Thesaurus iii; p. 1366; Raumer, Palastina, p. 174; (Alex.'s Kitto or McClintock and Strong's Cyclopaedia, under the word ).

STRONGS NT 108a: ἀηδίαἀηδία, (ας, ἡ (from ἀηδής, and this from the alpha privative and ἦδος pleasure, delight). (from Lysip. down);

1. unpleasantness, annoyance.

2. dislike, hatred: ἐν ἀηδία, manuscript Cantabr. in Luke 23:12 for Rec. ἐν ἔχθρα.
