Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 1160: δαπάνη

δαπάνη, δαπανης, ἡ (from δάπτω to tear, consume, (akin are δεῖπνον, Latindaps; Curtius, § 261)), expense, cost: Luke 14:28. (2 Esdr. 6:4; 1 Macc. 3:30, etc. Among Greek writings Hesiod, Works, 721, Pindar, Euripides, Thucydides, and following.)

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance
cost.

From dapto (to devour); expense (as consuming) -- cost.
