Strong's Concordance
titlos: a title, inscription
Original Word: τίτλος, ου, ὁ
Part of Speech: Noun, Masculine
Transliteration: titlos
Phonetic Spelling: (tit'-los)
Definition: Titius (surnamed Justus), a Christian
Usage: a title, inscription.
Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 5102: τίτλος

τίτλος, τιτλου, ὁ, a Latin word, a title; an inscription, giving the accusation or crime for which a criminal suffered: John 19:19, 20, and after it Ev. Nic c. 10, 1 at the end. (Sueton. Calig c. 32praecedente titulo qui causam poenae indicaret; again, Domit c. 10canibus objecit cunt hoe titulo: impie locutus parmularius.)
