Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 3043: λίνον

λίνον (Treg. λίνον (so R G in Matt. as below), incorrectly, for the iota ἰ is short; (cf. Lipsius, Gramm. Uutersuch., p. 42)), λινου, τό, the Sept. several times for פִּשְׁתָּה, in Greek writings from Homer down, flax: Exodus 9:31; linen, as clothing, Revelation 15:6 R G T Tr marginal reading; the wick of a lamp, Matthew 12:20, after Isaiah 42:3.

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance
linen.

Probably a primary word; flax, i.e. (by implication) "linen" -- linen.
