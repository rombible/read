Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 3697: ὁποῖος

ὁποῖος, ὁποια, ὁποῖόν (ποῖος with the relative ὁ), (from Homer down), of what sort or quality, what manner of: 1 Corinthians 3:13; Galatians 2:6; 1 Thessalonians 1:9; James 1:24; preceded by τοιοῦτος (such as), Acts 26:29.

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance
what manner of

From hos and poios; of what kind that, i.e. How (as) great (excellent) (specially, as an indefinite correlative to the definite antecedent toioutos of quality) -- what manner (sort) of, such as whatsoever.
