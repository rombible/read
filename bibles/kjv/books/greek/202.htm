Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 202: ἀκροατής

ἀκροατής, (οῦ, ὁ (ἀκροάομαι (see the preceding word)), a hearer: τοῦ νόμου, Romans 2:13; τοῦ λόγου, James 1:22f, 25. (Thucydides, Isocrates, Plato, Demosthenes, Plutarch.)

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance
hearer.

From akroaomai (to listen; apparently an intensive of akouo); a hearer (merely) -- hearer.
