Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 4511: ῤύσις

ῤύσις, ῤύσεως, ἡ (from an unused present ῤύὧ, from which several of the tenses of ῤέω are borrowed), a flowing, issue: τοῦ αἵματος, Mark 5:25; Luke 8:43 (on the two preceding passages, cf. Buttmann, § 147, 11; Winers Grammar, § 29, 3b.), 44 (Hippocrates, Aristotle).
