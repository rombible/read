Strong's Concordance
Alexandreus: an Alexandrian
Original Word: Ἀλεξανδρεύς, έως, ὁ
Part of Speech: Noun, Masculine
Transliteration: Alexandreus
Phonetic Spelling: (al-ex-and-reuce')
Definition: an Alexandrian
Usage: an Alexandrian, a native (or resident) of Alexandria in Egypt.

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 221: Ἀλεξανδρεύς

Ἀλεξανδρεύς, (έως, ὁ, an Alexandrian, a native or a resident of Alexandria (a celebrated city of Egypt): Acts 6:9; Acts 18:24. ((Plutarch, Pomp. 49, 6; others.))

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance
of Alexandria, Alexandrian.

From Alexandreia (the city so called); an Alexandreian or inhabitant of Alexandria -- of Alexandria, Alexandrian.
