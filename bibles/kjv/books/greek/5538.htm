Strong's Concordance
chrématismos: a divine response, an oracle
Original Word: χρηματισμός, οῦ, ὁ
Part of Speech: Noun, Masculine
Transliteration: chrématismos
Phonetic Spelling: (khray-mat-is-mos')
Definition: a divine response, an oracle
Usage: an oracle, divine response.
HELPS Word-studies

Cognate: 5538 xrēmatismós – a divine response (see xrēmatizō); "an oracular answer" (WS, 723), used only in Ro 11:4. See 5537 (xrēmatizō).

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 5538: χρηματισμός

χρηματισμός, χρηματισμοῦ, ὁ (χρηματίζω, which see), a divine response, an oracle: Romans 11:4. (2 Macc. 2:4; cf. Diodorus 1, 1; 14, 7; Clement of Rome, 1 Cor. 17, 5 [ET]; (cf. Artemidorus Daldianus, oneir. 1, 2, p. 8; Suicer, Thesaurus under the word (vol. 2, col. 1532)); in various other senses in the Greek writings from Xenophon, and Plato down.)
