Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 2368: θυμίαμα

θυμίαμα, θυμιάματος, τό (θυμιάω), the Sept. mostly for קְטֹרֶת, an aromatic substance burnt, incense: generally in plural, Revelation 5:8; Revelation 8:3; Revelation 18:13; ἡ ὥρα τοῦ Θεοῦ, when the incense is burned, Luke 1:10; θυσιαστήριον τοῦ θυμιάματος, Luke 1:11. (Sophocles, Herodotus, Aristophanes, Plato, Diodorus, Josephus; the Sept..)

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance
incense, odor.

From thumiao; an aroma, i.e. Fragrant powder burnt in religious service; by implication, the burning itself -- incense, odour.

see GREEK thumiao
