Word Origin

Definition

NASB Translation
flame (6), flaming (1).

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 5395: φλόξ

φλόξ, genitive φλογός, ἡ (φλέγω (to burn; cf. Latin 'flagro', etc.)), from Homer down, the Sept. for לַהַב and לֶהָבָה, a flame: Luke 16:24; on the phrases φλόξ πυρός and πῦρ φλογός see πῦρ, p. 558{a}.

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance
flame

From a primary phlego (to "flash" or "flame"); a blaze -- flame(-ing).
