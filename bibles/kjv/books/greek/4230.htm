Strong's Concordance
pragmateia: careful application, hard work
Original Word: πραγματεία, ας, ἡ
Part of Speech: Noun, Feminine
Transliteration: pragmateia
Phonetic Spelling: (prag-mat-i'-ah)
Definition: careful application, hard work
Usage: a business, an occupation, affair, transaction.
HELPS Word-studies

Cognate: 4230 pragmateía – a necessary daily (business) transaction. See 4231 (pragmateuomai).

Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 4230: πραγματεία

πραγματεία (T WH πραγματια; see Iota), πραγματείας, ἡ (πραγματεύομαι), prosecution of any affair; business, occupation: plural with the addition of τοῦ βίου, pursuits and occupations pertaining to civil life, opposed to warfare (A. V. the affairs of this life), 2 Timothy 2:4 (In the same and other senses in Greek writings from (Hippocrates), Xenophon, Plato down.)
