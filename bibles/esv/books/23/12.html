The Lord Is My Strength and My Song

1Youa will say in that day:
“I will give thanks to you, O Lord,
for though you were angry with me,
your anger turned away,
that you might comfort me.

2“Behold, God is my salvation;
I will trust, and will not be afraid;
for the Lord Godb is my strength and my song,
and he has become my salvation.”

3With joy youc will draw water from the wells of salvation. 4And you will say in that day:

“Give thanks to the Lord,
call upon his name,
make known his deeds among the peoples,
proclaim that his name is exalted.

5“Sing praises to the Lord, for he has done gloriously;
let this be made knownd in all the earth.
6Shout, and sing for joy, O inhabitant of Zion,
for great in youre midst is the Holy One of Israel.”

Footnotes:
a 1 The Hebrew for you is singular in verse 1
b 2 Hebrew for Yah, the Lord
c 3 The Hebrew for you is plural in verses 3, 4
d 5 Or this is made known
e 6 The Hebrew for your in verse 6 is singular, referring to the inhabitant of Zion
