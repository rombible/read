HANAMEL

han'-a-mel (hanam'el; the King James Version Hanameel, ha-nam'el):

The son of Shallum, Jeremiah's uncle, of whom the prophet, while in prison, during the time when Jerusalem was besieged by the Chaldeans, bought a field with due formalities, in token that a time would come when house and vineyards would once more be bought in the land (Jeremiah 32:6-15).
