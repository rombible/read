Pannag [E]

(sweet ), an article of commerce exported from Palestine to Tyre, ( Ezekiel 27:17 ) the nature of which is a pure matter of conjecture, as the term occurs nowhere else. A comparison of the passage in Ezekiel with ( Genesis 43:11 ) leads to the supposition that pannag represents some of the spices grown in Palestine.
