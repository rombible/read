Rib-lah [N] [E] [H]

(fertility ), One of the landmarks on the eastern boundary of the land of Israel, as specified by Moses. ( Numbers 34:11 ) It seems hardly possible, without entirely disarranging the specification or the boundary, that the Riblah in question can be the same with the following.

  1. Riblah in the land of Hamath, a place on the great road between Palestine and Babylonia, at which the kings of Babylonia were accustomed to remain while directing the operations of their armies in Palestine and Phoenicia. Here Nebuchadnezzer waited while the sieges of Jerusalem and of Tyre were being conducted by his lieutenants. ( Jeremiah 39:5 Jeremiah 39:6 ; Jeremiah 62:9 Jeremiah 62:10 Jeremiah 62:26 Jeremiah 62:27 ; 2 Kings 25:6 2 Kings 25:20 2 Kings 25:21 ) In like manner Pharaoh-necho after his victory over the Babylonians at Carchemish, returned to Riblah and summoned Jehoahaz from Jerusalem before him. ( 2 Kings 23:33 ) This Riblah still retains its ancient name, on the right (east) bank of the el-Asy (Orontes) upon the great road which connects Baalbek and Hums , about 36 miles northeast of the former end 20 miles southwest of the latter place.
