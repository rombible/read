ATTEND; ATTENDANCE

a-tend'; a-tend'-ans:

(1) "To incline," "listen," "regard" qashabh; (Psalms 17:1 etc.); then, in the King James Version, "observe," but in the Revised Version (British and American), more frequently, "give heed" prosechein noun, as in 1 Timothy 4:13.

(2) "To be with," "take care of," "wait upon" (Esther 4:5; Hebrews 7:13; Romans 13:6); literally, "give unremitting care to," as in 1 Corinthians 7:35 (Luther:

"serve the Lord constantly and without hindrance").
