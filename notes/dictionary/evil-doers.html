EVIL-DOERS

e-v'-l-doo'-erz mere`im; from ra`-a`; kakopoios, always plural:

Malefactors or offenders of God's law. Used generally of the ungodly, as, "Fret not thyself because of evil-doers." (Psalms 37:1). Sometimes also of personal offenders:

"He hath delivered the soul of the needy from the hand of evil-doers." (Jeremiah 20:13).
