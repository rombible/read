Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(25) Thick planks.--After stating that the doors just described were ornamented like the walls, the prophet speaks of something that was on the outer front of the porch. What this was, is extremely doubtful, as the word is elsewhere used only in 1Kings 7:6, of something in front of Solomon's cloisters or "porch of pillars." Perhaps the best suggestion is that it may have been a moulding of wood. The word in the original is in the singular.
